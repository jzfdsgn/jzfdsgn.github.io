Egman’s OP11 Half Case Tourney

June 11, 2025 at 11:36 AM

Yesterday Egman hosted his half case tourney, one of our first big OP11 events. While it’s not an official event, it’s still a good peek into the current playing field of the meta. Especially considering the sim hadn’t been updated with the new cards prior to the event.

Here’s the link to Egmans’s report.

It’s not surprising that UP Luffy and Belo Betty make up a majority of the pie chart. What is surprising is the diversity in the rest of the leaders played. And even more surprising is that Lucci won the whole event. The finals were Lucci vs. Belo Betty.

It’s notable that Lucci did not run into any Buggy’s the entire tournament, which is supposed to be a really bad matchup for the cat.

It gives me some hope to see BP Luffy with a strong showing, coming in 3rd. Not to mention there are 2 of them in the Top 8.

Now I feel even more unsure of what to play for Vegas. So I asked my buddy Claude (AI) to give me the conversion rates of each leader in the top 16. Check it out!

Now I’m strongly considering BP Luffy, but that means having to buy EB02 secret luffy. I could also just play Betty since I have the deck. Decisions, decisions..

Till next time,

OP11 Pre-releases are here!

May 27, 2025 at 9:12 PM

We’re going from one flavor of purple Luffy to another with OP11: A Fist of Divine Speed.

Pre-release events are starting this week, and we’re already seeing people rip some cases! This set seems to be just as polarizing as EB02 since we already know a certain purple rubber man is going to dominate the meta. But we’re also getting the new starter decks, which the east didn’t have for their OP11 meta.

I’m excited for it. I think we have a lot of players who like to cook unique decks to try and combat the meta. Not only that, we have a regional in Vegas only a few weeks into the set. Which is too soon to call it a solved meta when you consider the starter decks.

Whether you’re excited or in the OP12 waiting room, it’s always fun ripping packs. So if you’re playing in a pre-release event, I recommend this guide from Artress. He puts one out for every set.
